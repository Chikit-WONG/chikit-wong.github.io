About

I am an undergraduate student majoring in Artificial Intelligence at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou).

My current interests lie at the intersection of multimodal learning and intelligent interaction. I am particularly interested in building models that can connect perception, reasoning, and action, as well as exploring how neural signals can be aligned with modern vision-language representations.

I enjoy turning research ideas into reproducible systems, from model design and training to evaluation, analysis, and clear technical communication.

Selected Projects

Research and technical work that best represents my current direction.

Undergraduate Research Project 2026

Brain-to-Image Retrieval and Reconstruction via Multimodal EEG Alignment

A complete EEG-to-image system on the THINGS-EEG dataset, covering 200-way image retrieval and image reconstruction. The project aligns neural signals with visual embedding spaces and studies set-level constrained retrieval.

Research advisor: Chen Liang

Undergraduate Research Project 2025–2026

Spatially Enhanced Egocentric Visual Question Answering

Research on improving spatial understanding in egocentric visual question answering, with a focus on representing relationships among objects, viewpoints, and the surrounding scene.

Research advisor: Xuming Hu

Course Research Project 2025

Semantic Similarity Measurement in Latent Space for LLM Prediction Evaluation

An empirical study of embedding-based evaluation for language-model answers, comparing representation models such as BGE, MiniLM, and E5 and examining where cosine similarity succeeds or fails for longer responses.
